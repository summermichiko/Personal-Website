MY STORY

I’ve worked in education and nonprofits, and most recently, I opened a successful music and dance school with my husband in Honolulu, Hawaii. A year and a half later, we decided to sell the school and move to New York City for a job offer my husband couldn’t refuse - a student experience manager at a Manhattan startup. I was in the middle of applying for jobs, frustrated at how dishonest and political the whole process felt when my husband asked me to look through his company’s curriculum and give him feedback on how he could improve it for other students. The thought of doing anything on the computer dealing with coding was almost repulsive (I never understood how my husband could spend hours staring into his computer), but at this point, anything seemed better than continuing with my applications. So I did.
